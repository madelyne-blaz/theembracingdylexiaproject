Know the Signs

Common signs of dyslexia.

Early identification is key. Here are some signs to look for — by age and stage.

Preschool & Kindergarten

Difficulty learning nursery rhymes, trouble recognizing letters in their own name, delayed speech development.

Early Elementary

Trouble sounding out new words, slow or choppy reading, difficulty spelling even simple words.

Upper Elementary

Avoids reading aloud, struggles to read fluently, has trouble understanding what they read.

Middle & High School

Takes much longer to complete reading/writing tasks, poor spelling despite effort, trouble with foreign languages.

Adults

Reads slowly, avoids reading when possible, difficulty with writing and spelling, may have learned compensating strategies.

All Ages

Strong verbal skills but struggles on paper. Creative, bright, and capable, but reading feels like a fight.

Recommended Organizations

Organizations doing incredible work.

Hadley's picks

These are organizations doing incredible work in the dyslexia space. Whether you're a parent just starting out, a teacher looking for strategies, or someone who was just diagnosed — there's something here for you.

IDA
International Dyslexia Association
Nonprofit · Global

The leading organization dedicated to the study and treatment of dyslexia. The IDA provides fact sheets, a dyslexia handbook, structured literacy resources, and connections to local chapters — making it one of the most comprehensive starting points for anyone affected by dyslexia.

Parents Educators Advocacy Research
dyslexiaida.org Visit →
MBD
Made By Dyslexia
Global Charity · Led by Dyslexics

A global charity led by successful dyslexic people whose mission is to change how the world sees dyslexia — focusing on strengths, not just challenges. They offer free training on Microsoft Learn and LinkedIn Learning, a Dyslexic Thinking test, podcasts, and resources for schools and workplaces. Dyslexic Thinking is now an official skill on LinkedIn thanks to their work.

Parents Educators Advocacy Free Training
madebydyslexia.org Visit →
U
Understood
Nonprofit · Learning Differences

The leading nonprofit supporting the 70 million people in the US with learning and thinking differences. Understood offers expert-written articles, videos, interactive tools, and a free dyslexia fact sheet — all in plain language. Their "Through My Eyes" experience lets you see what daily life feels like for a child with dyslexia, ADHD, or dyscalculia.

Parents Educators Articles Videos
understood.org Visit →
Y
Yale Center for Dyslexia & Creativity
Research Center · Yale School of Medicine

Founded by world-renowned dyslexia scientists Drs. Sally and Bennett Shaywitz, the Yale Center is the leading source of cutting-edge research and science-backed resources on dyslexia. They offer a free 12-hour Coursera course on dyslexia, success stories of accomplished dyslexic adults, and practical tools for parents and educators. Their mission: illuminate the strengths — not just the challenges — of dyslexia.

Research Parents Free Course Success Stories
dyslexia.yale.edu Visit →
LDA
Learning Disabilities Association of America
Nonprofit · Since 1963

The largest member-based nonprofit advocating for individuals with learning disabilities — including dyslexia — since 1963. With over 100 state and local chapters, LDA provides support, education, and advocacy for nearly 3 million students and their families. A go-to source for understanding your legal rights, finding local support, and connecting with a national community.
