About Me

Hi, I'm Hadley.

Advocate, educator, Miss America Opportunity participant — and someone who believes every person with dyslexia deserves to feel seen, supported, and capable.

My Story

More than a pageant — a platform.

My name is Hadley. I'm a participant in the Miss America Opportunity — a scholarship program that empowers young women to make a real difference in their communities through a Community Service Initiative (CSI).

My CSI is titled Embracing Dyslexia. I advocate for, support, and teach about dyslexia so that every child and family facing it knows they're not alone — and that dyslexia doesn't define what they're capable of.

I've created hands-on dyslexia reading kits packed with tools to support struggling readers, and have distributed over 1,000 of them directly into the hands of families and classrooms in my community.

My Journey

How it all started.

A few milestones along the way.

The Beginning

I became passionate about dyslexia advocacy after seeing firsthand how many students struggle in silence — and how early awareness and the right tools can completely change a child's trajectory.

Embracing Dyslexia is Born

I launched my Community Service Initiative, building it around three pillars: advocate, support, and teach. The mission was clear — help the 1 in 5 stop struggling in silence.

1,000+ Reading Kits Distributed

I created dyslexia reading kits filled with tools to help support reading at home and in the classroom, and have been able to distribute over 1,000 of them in my community.

Miss America Opportunity

I joined the Miss America Opportunity as a way to amplify my platform, connect with more communities, and earn scholarship funding to continue growing Embracing Dyslexia.

Sharing My Message

I've visted schools, libaries, and even a caucus session, in the state of Washington as a part of an effort to educate individuals about the struggles of dyslexia. Additionally, I have created content on various social media platforms, giving me the opportunity to educate thousands of people across the nation on dylexia.

What I Stand For

My values.

These beliefs guide everything I do.

Every reader has potential.

Dyslexia is not a measure of intelligence. With the right tools and support, every person can thrive.

Early awareness changes everything.

The sooner families and educators recognize the signs of dyslexia, the sooner children can get the support they deserve.

No one should struggle in silence.

Community matters. Connection matters. No child — and no parent — should feel like they're navigating this alone.

Education is empowerment.

Understanding what dyslexia is (and isn't) removes shame, builds confidence, and opens doors.
